Undergraduate and graduate students are welcome to join the NCI lab!

  • You can participate in the NCI lab either as a research volunteer (min. of 4 hrs/week) OR by registering for the course SLHS 5980.
  • Contact suma.devanga@wmich.edu for more information.

What does it mean to be a part of this research lab?

  • New students will be involved in a brief training period by pairing up with experienced lab members.
  • You may be involved in collecting on-site data from participants with aphasia and other neurogenic communication disorders.
  • You may be involved in a variety of data analysis procedures including conducting transcription of recorded data, discourse analysis, data coding, and statistical analysis.
  • Based on your interests, you may be involved in presenting the research work at conferences.
  • You can also aim to do an undergraduate or masterâ€™s thesis!

In order to participate in the NCI lab, you must complete an online training module from the WMU HSIRB. Follow this link to the IRB Training Module.

  • Click on "register" in the upper right hand corner.
  • Find "Western Michigan University" in the dropdown box, and then continue to step 2 and so on.
  • You need to complete "Group 1 Social and Behavioral Sciences Researchers" which contains 12 modules. It should take approximately 2 hours to complete this training.
  • The training completion certificate should be emailed to the lab director at suma.devanga@wmich.edu.
